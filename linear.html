Curriculum

What is current day linear algebra:

  • A function is encoded in a matrix and linear algebra you can get all the statistics of this function
  • It's not even about matrices, it's about linear operators on functions
  • It's about surprising and important generalizations (of functions)
  • It's not about 'gaussian elimination' that's just a slightly better highschool technique
  • Linear algebra is about breaking up matrices into simpler pieces (SVD)
  • Vectors are about breaking them up into simpler subspaces and basis choices
  • Algebraic manipulations turn harder problems like differential eq or minimization problems into much simpler ones by doing algebra on whole matrices
  • Nonlinear equations can convert into a sequence of approximate linear equations whose solutions converge to the nonlinear solution (Newton's method from first year calculus)
  • In large matrices determinants play no role at all except to remind you about eigenvalues (which are also obsolete but we'll cover them)
  • We can find the roots of polynomials by converting them into a matrix and finding eigenvalues aka the companion matrix. A polynomial is also a model of a function.
  • Multiplying a matrix repeatedly is how you compute eignevectors/values.
  • Surprise: you don't need to know anything about eigenvalues to understand SVD it's a completely different topic
  • Linear algebra tells you about the 'structure' of the problem
  • Mean, variance/co-variance are dot products and projections AKA basic geometry
  • Linear algebra can be 3D+ aka multilinear algebra, using a higher dimensional matrix called the Kronecker product. In other word's partial differential equations and much more, recall a matrix is a function. Kronecker products can construct cartesian grids
